👆 Tap a card to hear the pronunciation

Ñ
niño
NEEN-yo

💡 Like the 'ny' in 'canyon' — press the middle of your tongue to the roof of your mouth.

RR
perro
PEH-rroh

💡 A trilled 'r' — try saying 'butter' fast and let your tongue flutter.

J
jamón
hah-MOHN

💡 Like a strong English 'h' — air from the back of the throat.

LL
llave
YAH-veh

💡 In most dialects sounds like the English 'y' in 'yes'.

V
vaca
BAH-kah

💡 In Spanish, V and B sound nearly identical — a soft 'b' sound.

Z
zapato
sah-PAH-toh

💡 In Latin American Spanish, sounds like 's' — not the English 'z'.

H
hola
OH-lah

💡 The H is always silent in Spanish — never pronounced.

G
gente
HEN-teh

💡 Before E or I, sounds like the Spanish J — a strong 'h' sound.
